The blog audience is CFOs and finance professionals. They already know AR collections, financial close, compliance, etc. Don't explain the domain to them — that's condescending.

Instead: show what AI agents and automation can do FOR them. Focus on benefits, time saved, risk reduced, decisions improved. Frame posts as "CFO living examples" — a series showing real automation value in finance scenarios they live every day.

**Why:** David's unique value is the intersection of finance domain expertise + AI engineering. The blog should speak to finance people in their language, not teach them their own job.

**How to apply:** Skip "if you've worked in finance, you know..." intros. Jump straight to what the agent does and why it matters. Use finance terminology naturally. Show the value, not the explanation.

**Voice:** Informal and conversational, like David speaks. Use contractions (it's, doesn't, can't). Short punchy sentences mixed with longer ones. Avoid editorial/literary framings like "Watch an X for a day and you'll see..." — just say what happens. Avoid corporate polish words (leverage, cutting-edge, revolutionize, robust, seamless).
